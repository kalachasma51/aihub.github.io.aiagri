Artificial Intelligence in Agriculture

Technology Introduced: Agricultural Production Management

Methodology Used:

The potential applications of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the agricultural industry, including crop production management, crop monitoring, data science, and disease detection. The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) has established a virtual Center of Excellence (COE) to provide strategic leadership on the application of AI in agricultural research, which includes transferring knowledge, sharing lessons learned, developing use cases, and expanding AI toolboxes

Explanation:

This article discusses the potential benefits of using Artificial Intelligence (AI) in agriculture, as a way to address the challenges presented by the growing world population, shrinking farmland, and climate change. The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) has established a virtual Center of Excellence (COE) to provide leadership on the application of AI in agricultural research. AI can be used in various areas of agricultural research, including production management, crop monitoring, data science, disease detection, and food quality.

Conclusion:

The application of Artificial Intelligence in agriculture has the potential to address the challenges posed by population growth, shrinking farmland, and climate change, among others. The research conducted by the United States Department of Agriculture and others highlighted in this Special Section demonstrate the promising role of AI in improving agricultural productivity, efficiency, and sustainability.

Reference:

S. Y. Liu, "USDA-ARS Washington, DC, USA," IEEE Transactions on Microwave Theory and Techniques, vol. 68, no. 7, pp. 3318-3325, Jul. 2020, doi: 10.1109/MITP.2020.2986121.
